Polanco, Mexico City

Back in colonial times, in the 16th century, the Hacienda de San Juan de los Morales was the first building in the area that the King of Spain donated to Hernan Cortes, and it was named after the mulberry trees that were planted there.

Around 1930, the lands were divided in response to the demand for urban areas in the city, which increased following the Mexican Revolution, as families wished to move away from the central area. The streets in the area were then named after different humanists, philosophers, and writers, including French author Eugenio Sue.

Cultural diversity has been a hallmark of the Polanco neighborhood. To this day, it is still the preferred area for new international residents, who are delighted by the cosmopolitan vibe and lively streets.
